# Depression Survey Data Description

This synthetic dataset simulates a cross-sectional survey study examining the relationship between modifiable lifestyle factors and depression severity. The data structure mirrors typical clinical psychology screening studies where participants complete the Beck Depression Inventory (BDI-II), a 21-item self-report questionnaire that measures the severity of depressive symptoms on a scale from 0 (minimal) to 63 (severe). Standard BDI-II clinical cutoffs classify scores into four severity levels: minimal (0-9), mild (10-18), moderate (19-28), and severe (29-63).

Alongside the BDI, participants reported on lifestyle and psychosocial variables known to be associated with depression: average nightly sleep duration, weekly exercise frequency, perceived social support quality, and subjective stress level. Demographic variables (age, gender, income bracket) were also collected. The data was generated to reflect established correlational patterns from the depression literature — shorter sleep, less exercise, weaker social support, and higher stress are associated with elevated BDI scores — while preserving realistic variance and avoiding deterministic relationships. This allows testing of both hypothesis-driven analyses (confirmatory regression) and data-driven approaches (variable selection, machine learning).
