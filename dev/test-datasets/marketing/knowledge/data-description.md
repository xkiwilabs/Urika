# Customer Segmentation Data Description

This synthetic dataset simulates a retail company's customer relationship management (CRM) database, designed for unsupervised customer segmentation analysis. Each record represents a unique customer with both demographic attributes (age, gender, education level, marital status) and behavioral metrics (annual income, spending score, purchase frequency). The spending score is a composite metric on a 1-100 scale that integrates transaction value, frequency, and recency — a simplified version of the RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) framework commonly used in marketing analytics.

The income variable follows a log-normal distribution to reflect real-world wealth concentration patterns, while the spending score is normally distributed and loosely correlated with income but includes substantial independent variance (representing individual spending habits beyond income effects). This structure is designed to produce meaningful clusters when analyzed with unsupervised methods such as k-means, hierarchical clustering, or DBSCAN. The expected outcome is discovery of 3-5 natural customer segments that differ in their demographic and behavioral profiles, enabling recommendations for differentiated marketing strategies (e.g., high-income low-spenders vs. moderate-income high-frequency buyers).
